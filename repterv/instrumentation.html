022-01 - Sensors and Instruments

Tachometer
​Single-phase AC generator tachometer
advantages:
  • ​the suppression of spurious signals due to a DC generator commutator (zavaró jelek elnyomása)
  • the independence of the information in relation to the airborne electrical power supply
 3-phase synchronous motor type tachometer
  • The frequency is proportional to the transmitter drive speed
  • The speed indicating element is a synchronous motor driving a magnetic tachometer
DC generator tachometer
  • easy transmission of the information
  • independence of the information relative to the electrical power supply
Three-phase electrical tachometer
  • a synchronous motor turning a drag cup.
  • consist of a three-phase generator, a synchronous motor and a magnetic tachometer.
  • operating principle to measure the rotation speed of a synchronous motor energized by an alternator
​Magnetic tachometer
  • consist of a permanent magnet turning inside a non magnetic drag cup.
Electronic tachometer sensor
  • a notched wheel rotating in front of an electro-magnet.
  • frequency of the electric impulse created by a notched wheel rotating in a magnetic field.
  • disadvantage: necessity of a power supply.
​The signal supplied by a transmitter fitted with a 3-phase AC generator, connected to RPM indicator, is
  • a three-phase voltage, the frequency of which varies with the RPM; the indicator is provided with a motor which drives a magnetic tachometer.
​The transmitter of RPM indicator may consist of
  • a magnetic sensor supplying an induced AC voltage
  • a three-phase AC generator supplying a three-phase voltage
EPR/EGT/CHT/RPM
The sensor(s) feeding the EPR-Engine Pressure Ratio-indicator
  • pressure probes, one located upstream from the compressor inlet, and the other downstream from the turbine outlet.

022-04 - Gyroscopic Instruments
DGI (directional gyro)
“Drift” = movement of the gyro spin axis along the horizontal plane,
“Topple” = movement of the gyro’s spin axis in the vertical plane.
Real wander: the 
  • spin axis actually moves in relation to its original fixed position in space
  • such as an imperfectly balanced gyroscope rotor
Apparent wander (Earth rate)
  • the observer rotates with the Earth
  • Rate of apparent DRIFT in ° per hour = 15° × sin (latitude); Apparent drift is zero at the equator and maximum (15° per hour) at the poles. (Turns right N and left S)
    • Rate of apparent TOPPLE in ° per hour = 15° × cos (latitude); Apparent topple is zero at the poles and maximum (15° per hour) at the equator. (Turns right N and left S)
Transport wander
  • has the same effect as the rotation of the Earth. 
  • ​happens when the gyro is attached to a structure (for example an aircraft) that changes its position on Earth in the east-west direction
DRIFT NORTH: sin lat *15 right
DRIFT SOUTH: sin lat*15 left
