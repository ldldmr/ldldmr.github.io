LOT
  • 73 AC
  • In cooperation with NORDIC Aviation Group
    • Nordica aircraft: Bombardier CRJ 900 ERs
  • Polskie Linie Lotnicze LOT
  • Flag carrier of Poland, national airline
  • Estabilished in Dec 29. 1928,  one of the world's oldest airlines
  • Home base Warsaw Chopin: HUB
  • More than 100 destinations
  • Europe, the Middle East, North America, and Asia
  • Long haul flights
    • ​New York City, Newark, Chicago, Los Angeles, Toronto, Seoul, Singapore, Beijing, and Tokyo
    • From Budapest base New York, Chicago
  • Economy, Premium Economy and Business class
  • member of the Star Alliance.
  • CEO: Rafał Milczarski
  • Company slogan: You choose the direction
  • LOT’s strategy is based on five pillars
    • ​taking advantage of growing market
    • developing a network of connections and a hub
    • competition
    • boosting effectiveness
    • building a committed team

Why LOT
  • LOT is one of the world's oldest airlines and greatest airlines in Europe. With it's significant fleet size and long-haul flights it has a good reputation and outstanding performance during this long period. There is the opportunity here to fly the Boeing family from small to big, which predicts a good chance for carrier advancement. A national airline always offers a more stable job and working environment, this is why I definitely choose LOT for my Nr1 airline.
  • I remember the flight when I traveled to Canada with LOT to my advanced flight training. At that time I knew it is a different airline, I felt comfortable, calmness and proud to fly with them.
What you don't like about your current job
  • It is definitely the very few flight time. My job could be one of the best jobs in the world, but nowadays we have more administration and military type of work than flying. However the flying part is very complex and there is continuous pressure put on the pilots to complete their missions which should comply with flight times. I feel I need new challenges and I hope I will find them being an airline pilot.
What are your weaknesses
  • Sometimes I end up taking too much on and when I am deeply involved my work I often forget about family programs. A good example for this is the upcoming parachute jump world championship at Szolnok in which I take part as an organizer and probably spend my whole day with the teams.
  • I like to say yes to extra work if it seems to be challenging, and I forget about family programs.
  • Sometimes I am too loyal to the people or organisation I belong to, not recognizing the weaknesses of it.
